Data Analytics

Introduction

Data Analytics is the science of examining raw data to uncover patterns, draw conclusions, and support decision-making. Every company today collects massive amounts of data — from customer behavior and sales figures to website traffic and social media metrics. Data analysts are the professionals who turn this raw data into actionable insights that drive business strategy.

Our Data Analytics program teaches you how to collect, clean, analyze, and visualize data using industry-standard tools and techniques.

What is Data Analytics?

Data analytics is the process of examining data sets to draw conclusions about the information they contain. It involves applying statistical analysis and logic to extract meaningful patterns. Data analytics helps businesses optimize performance, improve decision-making, identify market trends, and better understand customer needs. It is the foundation of the data-driven decision-making approach used by modern organizations.

"Data is the new oil. It's valuable, but if unrefined it cannot really be used. Data analytics is the refining process."

History & Significance

Data analytics has roots in statistics and mathematics dating back centuries, but the modern field emerged in the 1960s with the advent of computers and databases. The term "Big Data" gained prominence in the 2000s as companies like Google, Amazon, and Facebook began processing massive datasets. Today, data analytics is essential across all industries — from healthcare and finance to sports and entertainment — and data analyst roles consistently rank among the top growing jobs worldwide.
